MENTAL HEALTH awareness
Mental health awareness is a critical component of overall well-being, emphasizing the importance of understanding and addressing mental health issues. It involves promoting understanding, empathy, and support for individuals facing mental health challenges, reducing stigma, and encouraging open conversations. Raising mental health awareness helps recognize that mental health is as important as physical health, fostering a holistic approach to healthcare.

Recognize Symptoms

Self-identifying your own mental health status can be a valuable first step toward seeking guidance and support. However, it's important to remember that self-assessment is not a substitute for professional diagnosis or treatment.
Learn about common mental health symptoms and disorders, such as depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and more. Recognize if you are experiencing any of these symptoms. Keep a journal to track your emotions and thoughts over time.

Self-Assessment Tools:

Various online mental health self-assessment tools and questionnaires are available. These can provide a preliminary indication of potential mental health concerns. However, they should not replace professional evaluation

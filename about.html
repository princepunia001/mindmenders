About Self-Identifying Mental Health

What is stress?

Stress is a natural physiological and psychological response that occurs when an individual perceives a threat, challenge, or demand in their environment. It's a normal part of life and can be triggered by various situations and events, both positive and negative. Stress serves as a survival mechanism, preparing the body to react to potential threats or challenges. When faced with stress, the body releases hormones like cortisol and adrenaline, which can lead to a range of physical and mental responses.

What is mental health?

Mental health refers to a person's emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It encompasses various aspects of an individual's life, including their thoughts, feelings, behaviors, relationships, and overall mental functioning. Good mental health is characterized by the ability to cope with the challenges and stresses of life, build and maintain positive relationships, work productively, make informed decisions, and adapt to changes.

To cure stress!

It's important to clarify that mental health conditions are not typically "cured" in the same way that a bacterial infection might be treated with antibiotics. Mental health conditions are complex and can vary greatly in terms of severity and impact on an individual's life. Instead of "curing" mental health conditions, the focus is on managing symptoms, improving well-being, and enhancing an individual's quality of life.
